**Research**


**Completing robot code by adding an if statement**
If statements are good to check conditions of the robot. This way, if the condition of the robot's path is positive for it to turn right it will turn right iof not then he next code segment tells the robot to move forward. So it will not stop after turning right when it needs to. 

**Key Benefits**

The benefits are allowing great efficiency because every single movement does not need to be coded separately, reduces confusion, and also allows testing on different paths for the robot. 

**Implementation Examples**

Robot vacuums are robots that vacuum the houses of individuals. When faced with obstacles like walls, furniture, or people they are designed in a similar if statement to manuver avoiding the obstacle and continuing with their path. This way they can reach their goal and destination with no obstacles on the way.
